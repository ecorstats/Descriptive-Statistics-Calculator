## Understanding Descriptive Statistics

To analyze data effectively, one must understand **descriptive statistics**. These statistics summarize and describe the main features of a dataset.

### What Are Descriptive Statistics?

Descriptive statistics provide a quick overview of data. They include measures such as:

- Mean  
- Median  
- Mode  
- Range  
- Standard deviation  

These measures help describe both the **central value** and the **spread** of the data.

---

## Types of Descriptive Statistics

There are two main types of descriptive statistics:

### 1. Measures of Central Tendency
These describe the middle or typical value of a dataset.

- [**Mean**](https://ecorstats.com/average-calculator-statistical-analysis-in-2025/)
- **Median**
- **Mode**

### 2. Measures of Variability
These show how spread out the data are.

- **Range**
- **Variance**
- **Standard Deviation**

## Interpreting Results from the Descriptive Statistics Calculator

Once calculations are complete, it is important to understand the results.

---

### Understanding Measures of Central Tendency

These measures describe what is typical in your dataset.

#### Mean, Median, and Mode

- **Mean**: Average of all values  
- **Median**: Middle value when data are ordered  
- **Mode**: Most frequently occurring value  

Each measure highlights a different aspect of the data.

---

### When to Use Each Measure

- Use **mean** for normally distributed data  
- Use **median** when data contain outliers  
- Use **mode** to identify the most common value  

---

## Making Sense of Dispersion Measures

Dispersion measures explain how spread out the data are.

### Standard Deviation and Variance

- **Standard deviation** shows how far values deviate from the mean  
- **Variance** is the square of the standard deviation  

Both are critical for understanding data variability.

---

### Range, Quartiles, and IQR

- **Range**: Difference between highest and lowest values  
- **Quartiles**: Divide data into four equal parts  
- **Interquartile Range (IQR)**: Middle 50% of the data  

These measures help visualize data spread and detect outliers.
